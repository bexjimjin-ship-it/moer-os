# Order Center Roadmap

## Roadmap Principle

Build Order Center in phases.

Do not try to automate everything in the first version.

First make the work visible.

Then make it faster.

Then make it intelligent.

## Phase 1: Foundation

### Goal

Create the basic order tracking structure.

### Scope

- Customer records
- Inquiry records
- Product records
- Supplier records
- Quotation records
- Purchase order records
- Basic status tracking
- Notes
- Timeline

### Success Measure

Jim can see all active orders and know the next action for each one.

## Phase 2: Production and Inspection

### Goal

Track supplier progress and quality control.

### Scope

- Production status
- Expected completion dates
- Supplier update reminders
- Inspection planning
- Inspection result records
- Photo and video references
- Customer approval status

### Success Measure

Moer can identify delays and quality issues before shipment.

## Phase 3: Warehouse and Consolidation

### Goal

Manage physical goods after supplier delivery.

### Scope

- Warehouse receiving
- Carton count
- Weight and volume
- Missing goods
- Damaged goods
- Consolidation groups
- Packing list preparation

### Success Measure

Moer can clearly see which goods are received, missing, and ready to ship.

## Phase 4: Shipment and Documents

### Goal

Track shipping and keep documents organized.

### Scope

- Shipment records
- Container records
- Forwarder information
- ETD and ETA
- Tracking number
- Shipping documents
- Loading photos and videos
- Customer shipment updates

### Success Measure

Every shipment has clear status, documents, and customer update history.

## Phase 5: Payments

### Goal

Track money clearly.

### Scope

- Customer deposit
- Customer balance
- Supplier deposit
- Supplier balance
- Shipping cost
- Service fee
- Payment proof
- Payment reminders

### Success Measure

Moer can quickly see what has been paid and what is still due.

## Phase 6: AI Assistant

### Goal

Use structured order data to reduce repetitive work.

### Possible AI Features

- Extract inquiry details from WhatsApp or email
- Summarize order status
- Draft customer updates
- Draft supplier follow-up messages
- Detect overdue supplier updates
- Detect production delay risk
- Compare supplier quotations
- Generate inspection checklists
- Summarize inspection reports
- Find missing documents
- Suggest next actions

### Success Measure

Jim spends less time searching and writing repeated updates.

## Phase 7: Advanced Operations

### Goal

Make Order Center stronger for larger volume.

### Expansion Ideas

- Supplier performance scoring
- Customer value scoring
- Profit and margin tracking
- Multi-currency handling
- Multi-language customer updates
- Product category database
- Reorder tracking
- Recurring customer order templates
- Container loading optimization
- Forwarder comparison
- Document OCR
- WhatsApp conversation summaries
- Email integration
- Calendar reminders
- Mobile photo upload
- Role permissions

## Suggested Build Order

1. Order project dashboard
2. Inquiry and product capture
3. Supplier options and quotation comparison
4. Purchase order tracking
5. Production follow-up
6. Inspection records
7. Warehouse receiving
8. Consolidation
9. Shipment tracking
10. Payment tracking
11. Documents
12. AI helper

## What Not To Build First

Avoid building these too early:

- Complex accounting
- Full ERP features
- Advanced permissions
- Deep shipping integrations
- Automated supplier scoring
- Complex AI workflows
- Customer login portal

These may be useful later, but they are not needed for the first strong version.

## Long-Term Vision

Order Center should become the trusted operating record for every Moer Sourcing order.

It should help the team work faster, communicate better, reduce mistakes, and build stronger long-term customer relationships.
